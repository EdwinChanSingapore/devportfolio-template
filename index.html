About Me

Bachelors of Science (Hons w Highest Distinction) from the National University of Singapore, major in Life Sciences, minors in Computer Science and Physics. Also a graduate of the University's Scholars Programme.

I am interested in solving problems using data, and my key research has been the application of machine learning tools to solve novel problems in bioinformatics. I am currently working on building a software platform to enable clinical personal genomics, using a machine learning framework for analysis of variant call data and a bayesian inference network to perform mutation ranking.

Other roles I have taken on include strategy analysis at the Prime Minister's Office (Strategy Group) as an intern, and data analytics as a research assistant at the Institute of Water Policy.
